Tennis

With there being 3 Lawn Tennis Courts available, you really can't ask for more when it comes to displaying your fine tennis skills on court.

The club has nine clay and one synthetic court along with a tennis pavilion. Five of the courts have floodlights, which can be used after sunset by prior booking. One of the courts is dedicated for training purpose, where an experienced trainer is available to provide coaching to new players. An experienced marker is also available. After the game, players can relax in the tennis lounge. Various local, national and international events have been held here.

Famous Player


Roger Federer

Widely regarded as the greatest tennis player of all time, Roger Federer has won the most number of men's Grand Slam singles titles. He is the only person to be honored with the BBC Overseas Sports Personality of the Year award on four occasions. Despite enduring several injuries, Federer continues to dominate the tennis court.

Rules

Here is a simplified rundown of the rules of tennis:

  • A ball must land within bounds for play to continue; if a player hits the ball outside of bounds, this results in the loss of the point for them.
  • Players/teams cannot touch the net or posts or cross onto the opponent's side.
  • Players/teams cannot carry the ball or catch it with the racquet.
  • Players cannot hit the ball twice.
  • Players must wait until the ball passes the net before they can return it.
  • A player that does not return a live ball before it bounces twice loses the point.
  • If the ball hits or touches the players, that counts as a penalty.
  • If the racquet leaves the hand or verbal abuse occurs, a penalty is given.
  • Any ball that bounces on the lines of boundary are considered good.
  • A serve must bounce first before the receiving player can return it.
