Skating

It's time to have fun and frolic. It's time to move on wheels. Skating has never been more fun at RYAN sports club.

Skating, recreational and competitive sport in which the participants use special shoes fitted with small wheels to move about on rinks or paved surfaces. Roller-skating sports include speed skating, hockey, figure skating, and dancing competitions similar to the ice-skating sports, as well as the vertical and street-style competitions common to so-called extreme sports.

Famous Player

Nancy Kerrigan

Figure skater Nancy Kerrigan is best known for her 1993 U.S. Figure Skating Championship win. She has also won medals at the 1991 and 1992 World Championships and the 1992 and 1994 Winter Olympics. She was attacked and injured by a man hired by her rival Tonya Harding's former husband.

Rules

Here is a simplified rundown of the rules of skating:

  • Socks must be worn with Rental Skates.
  • Skaters utilizing skate mates are also asked to stay toward the center of the skating floor. No standing on skate mates. All skaters must skate carefully and with the flow of traffic at all times. No climbing over barrier walls. When exiting the skating surface, look for an opening and exit very carefully!
  • No sitting on walls, tables, or counters.
  • No stopping or standing on the skating floor. Unruly or dangerous skaters will be asked to leave the rink with no refund.
  • Skating is not recommended for guests with asthma, epilepsy, seizure disorders.
  • Do not wear skates while entering or exiting the building or in the bathrooms.
  • Cell phones, earbuds, headphones, tablets, or similar devices cannot be used while skating.
  • Parents are not allowed to carry children while they are wearing skates for obvious safety reasons!
  • All inline and quad skates must be clean and in good repair. No missing wheels, no missing stops, or sharp edges.
  • When you finish skating please tuck laces inside the rental skates and return them to the rental desk.
